# Taylor County / Foley Cellulose — Evidence Investigation

A **facts-only** evidence file examining whether Taylor County, Florida has an abnormal cancer
burden and whether the former **Foley Cellulose** pulp mill / **Fenholloway River** discharge /
**Perry municipal water** could be a contributing exposure. Assembled to support a possible
**class action**.

> **This is a research organizer, not legal advice, and not a conclusion.** Nothing here asserts
> the mill caused any cancer. Retain a licensed Florida environmental / toxic-tort attorney and
> qualified experts (epidemiologist, toxicologist, hydrogeologist). The case-dispositive records
> come through public-records requests and discovery.

## How to read the tags

**Verification status**
- ✅ **Verified** — corroborated this session by ≥1 independent, credible source.
- 🟡 **Reported — unverified** — plausible and consistent, but the exact figure/ID was not confirmed here; pull the named source.
- 🟠 **Disputed / mislabeled** — the prior framing compares unlike things or is imprecise; needs a like-for-like re-pull. **Not "false."**
- ⛔ **Not established** — not supported by available data (suppressed, unmeasured, or a causation leap).

**Source tier** (for litigation, the source matters as much as the fact)
- **Tier 1** — primary agency record (authenticable / potentially admissible): FDEP, EPA, CCR, cancer registry, court dockets.
- **Tier 2** — secondary reporting (a lead, generally hearsay): news, NGO releases, Wikipedia.
- **Tier 3** — prior AI-thread assertion; verify against Tier 1 before any filing.

## Method & honest limitations

- Facts were gathered by **web search** and cross-checked against multiple independent sources.
  Where verification was possible, the prior research thread's **core facts held up**; the flags
  mark "confirm against the source document," **not** "this is false."
- **This environment could not directly open** primary PDFs or JavaScript-rendered database tables
  (State Cancer Profiles, the City of Perry CCR, FDEP Oculus, EPA ECHO). Figures behind those are
  flagged 🟡/🟠 and the exact URLs are provided so an attorney can pull them as **Tier-1** records.
- The single most load-bearing cancer statistic (Taylor County lung 65.0/100k) surfaced as
  **late-stage** incidence while its comparators were **all-stages** — a mismatch that must be
  resolved with an all-stages re-pull before it is used anywhere.
- **Confounders** the original thread omitted are flagged: the county's small population
  (~21,796 → rate instability + suppression) and **smoking** (the dominant lung-cancer driver,
  which must be adjusted for before any environmental attribution).

## Strongest vs. weakest (candid)

- **Strongest / well documented:** decades of permitted ~50 MGD dioxin-bearing discharge; the 1990
  EPA ~1,900× dioxin finding; the official **"DO NOT EAT" fish advisory**; the 1947 Class-V
  "sacrifice" and the 1995/2003 discharge-relocation decisions; the still-active 2026 regulation.
- **Weakest / currently unproven:** **causation** (discharge → specific plaintiffs' cancers); the
  **mill → municipal-water** pathway (the 2024 TTHM violation is a chlorination byproduct, not mill
  effluent); and a **statistically defined cancer cluster** (small-population power limits).
